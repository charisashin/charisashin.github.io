VISIONS:
Asian-American Storytelling

Overview

VISIONS is a publication that celebrates the diversity of Brown and RISD's Asian/Asian-American and Pacific Islander (AAPI) community. I currently serve as the magazine's web designer.

Tasked with the job of designing and developing the magazine's website, hosted at visions_mag.com, I set off on finding the best way to weave together elements that would represent the publication's unique brand and collection of AAPI literary and artistic voices.

My Role

Web Designer and Developer

Translating paper to pixels

As the Web Designer and Developer, I designed, prototyped, and developed the website for VISIONS, crafting a distinct visual identity through the layout and thematic elements to represent the magazine's mission of giving voice to Asian and Asian-American artists and writers.

Style Guide

In developing the style guide for the website, I chose a palette that reflected the current and previous print issues of the magazine. VISIONS tends to favor art pieces that dip into issues dealing with identity, background and heritage, and reconciling cultural roots, so I tried to echo those themes in the colors I chose.

Takeaways & Moving Forward

1. Next steps. This semester I will focus on optimizing the site for mobile, interviewing with the magazine's alumni for the History page, and creating pages for Events and Past Issues.

2. Reflections. I joined VISIONS in my sophomore year of my undergraduate studies and have since benefitted immensely from my work with the magazine. Not only was I able to fulfill a long-lasting passion of mine in sharing untold stories about the Asian and Asian-American experience, I was given creative liberties in shaping the website to reflect how I envisioned visual design could best convey the mission of the magazine.
