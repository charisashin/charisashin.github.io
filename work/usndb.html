Overview

The U.S. Network for Democracy in Brazil (USNDB) is a decentralized, democratic, non-partisan national network focused on educating the U.S public about Brazil and defending progressive social change.

I led a project to redesign the network's website as a part of the NGO's rebrand. My role was to lead the team through the phases of the design process, establish our design system, and partner cross-functionally on strategy and implementation. As the project is still in progress, this case study focuses primarily on the finished home page.

Defining Goals

In order to revamp the network's identity, I defined concrete objectives with my supervisor and team, including: create a new digital design system to reflect our new brand identity and rethink the narrative of key pages of the website based off an expanded audience.

Determining Deliverables

Based on the insights gleaned from the task analysis exercise, I collaborated with my team (consisting of my manager, a web content editor, and a social media manager) to narrow down our previous goals into deliverable steps.

1. Reorganize how information is displayed.
We needed to move away from relying on a slider to instead establishing a logical heirarchy of information on the home page.

2. Incorporate more imagery and kill needless space.
Users had trouble differentiating unique website sections because of the sections' similarity and unintentional cohesion. Images and effective use of whitespace could counter these issues.

3. Refresh the branding identity.
In order to reach a more diverse audience, the Network required an overall revamp of graphical components, colors, typography, and logo.
