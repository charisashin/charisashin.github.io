Here are a few other projects, ideas, and random things I've worked on over the years. Some are academic-related, some are just for fun.


DESIGN AT BROWN

ENGN 32 Course Creation

October 2019 - present
Ongoing Project

Design at Brown is a student group working to advance curriculum, information, and opportunities for students interested in design at Brown University.

My role in the group, beginning October 2019, was to assist in building ENGN 32: Intro to Design, a course in Brown's introductory sequence within the engineering department. I helped shape the curriculum by brainstorming project ideas, communicating with the faculty instructor, and writing the labs for the course. Taught by Professor Michael Donohue, ENGN 32 is currently projected to launch in Fall 2020.





CORNERSTONE MAGAZINE

Web Design & Online Content for Cornerstone Magazine

July 2019-present
Ongoing Project

Cornerstone is Brown and RISD's Christian literary magazine.

From the mission statement: Cornerstone is a publication that celebrates the truth and beauty of the Christian Gospel in order to glorify God, edify the Church, and reach the non-believer. We aim to provoke spiritual thought on and around the campuses of Brown and RISD. We publish works of art, prose, and poetry of all denominational persuasions that exhibit intelligent and creative approaches to current events, history, and our Christian faith.


I also cover content maintenance for the blog pieces!




DEMOCRACY IN BRAZIL

Web Design and Identity for Democracy in Brazil

May 2019 - present
Ongoing Project

An advocacy and organizational website created for Brown University's Brazilian Studies department in conjunction with the U.S. Network for Democracy in Brazil. I designed the UI display to reflect the national identity of Brazil and the nation's rich and vibrant culture. This site serves as the flagship center of updates from multiple Brazilian working groups focusing on issues like academic freedom, state violence, and universal healthcare access.

The website was soft-launched at the Challenges to Democracy in Brazil conference at Brown University in May 2019, but is still under construction.
